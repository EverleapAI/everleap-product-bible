# Everleap Product Principles

These principles guide every product decision made within Everleap.

Whenever uncertainty exists about a feature, prompt, page, recommendation, or piece of AI-generated content, these principles take precedence.

---

# Principle 1: People Are Becoming

Everleap assumes people are continuously developing.

No assessment should imply a person's identity is complete.

Every insight should acknowledge that growth, experience, and changing circumstances can reshape who someone becomes.

The product should describe emerging patterns rather than permanent characteristics.

---

# Principle 2: Evidence Before Interpretation

Observations should be grounded in evidence.

Evidence includes:

- Story responses
- User interactions
- Completed actions
- Reflection history
- Science memos
- Longitudinal behavioral patterns

Interpretation always comes after evidence.

Guidance always comes after interpretation.

Recommendations always come last.

---

# Principle 3: Curiosity Over Certainty

Curiosity creates growth.

Certainty often stops it.

Everleap intentionally uses language that reflects uncertainty when uncertainty exists.

Preferred language:

- You may notice...
- One possibility is...
- This appears to...
- It isn't clear yet whether...
- A pattern worth exploring...

Avoid language such as:

- You are...
- You always...
- You definitely...
- This proves...

---

# Principle 4: Patterns Over Biography

Everleap should never retell a person's life.

Insight is not biography.

Bad:

"You built a fencing academy."

Good:

"You seem energized when creating structure from complexity."

Science should abstract recurring patterns rather than summarizing life events.

---

# Principle 5: Development Over Labels

Labels simplify people.

Development explains people.

Everleap should describe:

- behaviors
- tendencies
- motivations
- environments
- conditions
- growth

Never identity.

---

# Principle 6: Reflection Before Advice

Reflection creates ownership.

Advice creates dependence.

Everleap should encourage users to think before telling them what to do.

Questions are often more valuable than answers.

---

# Principle 7: Small Actions Beat Big Plans

Growth rarely happens because of a single insight.

Growth happens through repeated small actions.

Every recommendation should feel achievable.

Small wins create momentum.

Momentum creates confidence.

---

# Principle 8: Every Science Has One Job

Each scientific framework answers exactly one question.

Science should never overlap unnecessarily.

Each contributes one perspective.

The synthesis happens later.

No science owns the truth.

---

# Principle 9: Summary Is a Synthesis

The Summary page is not another assessment.

It exists to identify one meaningful cross-science hypothesis.

Its purpose is to encourage exploration of the deeper Insights pages.

Summary should never:

- assign identity
- predict careers
- retell biography
- duplicate the science pages

---

# Principle 10: AI Is a Collaborator

Artificial intelligence exists to organize information.

Not replace human judgment.

AI should:

- synthesize
- recognize patterns
- explain evidence
- ask thoughtful questions
- encourage exploration

AI should never pretend to possess certainty it does not have.

---

# Principle 11: Every Screen Answers One Question

Every screen has a single purpose.

If a screen attempts to answer multiple unrelated questions, the experience becomes confusing.

Simple pages create trust.

Focused pages create clarity.

---

# Principle 12: Confidence Measures Evidence

Confidence does not measure truth.

Confidence measures how much supporting evidence currently exists.

Low confidence is not failure.

It is an invitation to continue learning.

---

# Principle 13: Teen First

Everleap is designed primarily for high school students.

Writing should be:

- intelligent
- conversational
- respectful
- encouraging

Never:

- corporate
- clinical
- patronizing
- exaggerated

Young people should feel understood, not talked down to.

---

# Principle 14: Explainability

Every important observation should be explainable.

If an engineer, counselor, or user asks:

"Why did Everleap say that?"

There should be evidence supporting the observation.

The product should never produce mysterious conclusions.

---

# Principle 15: Preserve Agency

Everleap should increase freedom.

Not reduce it.

Recommendations should expand possibilities.

Not narrow them.

The user always remains responsible for their own choices.

Everleap supports decisions.

It does not make them.

---

# Principle 16: Build Trust Slowly

Trust is earned.

Every interaction should make Everleap slightly more trustworthy.

Trust grows when the product:

- admits uncertainty
- remembers accurately
- avoids exaggeration
- stays consistent
- respects evidence

Protecting trust is more important than sounding impressive.

---

# Final Principle

Whenever two possible product decisions exist, choose the one that:

- encourages curiosity
- respects uncertainty
- preserves agency
- supports reflection
- helps the user understand themselves more deeply

That choice will almost always be the more enduring one.
