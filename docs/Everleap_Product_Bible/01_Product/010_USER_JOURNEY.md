# User Journey

## Purpose

Everleap is not designed to be completed.

It is designed to be lived.

Unlike a traditional assessment that produces a report and ends, Everleap becomes more valuable the longer someone uses it.

The product is a continuous journey of self-discovery, reflection, experimentation, and growth.

Every stage of the journey should naturally lead to the next.

---

# The Journey

The Everleap experience consists of six major phases.

The user may revisit these phases many times.

They are not linear.

They are cyclical.

Each cycle should improve understanding.

---

# Phase 1 — Welcome

The user arrives knowing very little about Everleap.

Their first question is usually:

> Why should I care?

Our responsibility is not to explain the technology.

Our responsibility is to create curiosity.

The onboarding experience should feel like the beginning of an interesting conversation rather than the start of an assessment.

---

# Phase 2 — Discovery

The user begins answering questions.

These questions are not tests.

They are opportunities to collect meaningful evidence.

Every question should have a purpose.

Every answer should contribute to understanding.

The user should feel listened to rather than evaluated.

---

# Phase 3 — Understanding

As enough evidence accumulates, Everleap begins recognizing patterns.

Scientific frameworks interpret the evidence independently.

The product then combines those perspectives into thoughtful observations.

These observations remain hypotheses.

The goal is not certainty.

The goal is reflection.

---

# Phase 4 — Daily Growth

Self-understanding has little value unless it changes daily life.

The Today experience exists to bridge understanding and action.

Every day the product should encourage one small step.

One reflection.

One experiment.

One opportunity to notice something new.

Growth happens through repetition.

---

# Phase 5 — Exploration

As confidence increases, users begin exploring possibilities.

Not careers.

Possibilities.

Subjects.

Projects.

Activities.

Communities.

Skills.

Experiences.

The product should help users discover opportunities aligned with emerging patterns.

It should never narrow possibilities too early.

---

# Phase 6 — Evolution

People change.

Everleap expects this.

New experiences create new evidence.

Old assumptions become outdated.

Patterns strengthen.

Others disappear.

The product should continuously update its understanding rather than protecting earlier conclusions.

Growth is expected.

---

# The Journey Never Ends

Everleap is not designed around completion.

There is no finish line.

Instead the relationship deepens over time.

Every conversation improves understanding.

Every action creates evidence.

Every reflection improves future guidance.

The product should become increasingly personal without becoming increasingly certain.

---

# Success

A successful journey looks like this:

A new user becomes curious.

Curiosity becomes participation.

Participation creates evidence.

Evidence creates understanding.

Understanding encourages action.

Action creates new evidence.

The cycle repeats.

Each cycle leaves the user with greater confidence, not because Everleap has told them who they are, but because they increasingly understand themselves.

---

# Design Principles

The user journey should always feel:

- Continuous
- Encouraging
- Curious
- Evidence-based
- Personal
- Flexible

It should never feel:

- Linear
- Judgmental
- Complete
- Finished
- Deterministic

Everleap should always leave room for the next chapter of a person's life.
