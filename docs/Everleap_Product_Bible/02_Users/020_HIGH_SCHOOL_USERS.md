# High School Users

## Purpose

High school students are the primary audience for Everleap.

Every major product decision should first consider whether it improves the experience for this group.

While Everleap may eventually serve users throughout adulthood, the product is intentionally designed around the developmental needs of teenagers between approximately ages 14 and 18.

Everything from the language, visual design, pacing, recommendations, and AI interactions should assume this audience first.

---

# Understanding the User

Most high school students are in the middle of building their identity.

They are not looking for confirmation.

They are looking for understanding.

Many are asking questions such as:

- What am I good at?
- What should I do after high school?
- Why do some things excite me while others don't?
- Why do I seem different from my friends?
- What kind of future would actually make me happy?

Very few expect an app to answer those questions.

Many simply want help thinking through them.

---

# Their Reality

High school students live in a world filled with expectations.

Parents ask about college.

Teachers ask about grades.

Friends compare themselves constantly.

Social media encourages comparison.

Schools often emphasize achievement more than self-understanding.

Many students feel pressure to choose a direction before they understand themselves.

Everleap exists to reduce that pressure rather than increase it.

---

# What They Need

They need permission to explore.

They need language for experiences they haven't yet described.

They need confidence that uncertainty is normal.

They need opportunities to discover strengths they may not recognize.

They need guidance without being controlled.

Most importantly, they need someone—or something—that assumes they are still becoming.

---

# What They Do NOT Need

They do not need another personality label.

They do not need another career quiz.

They do not need another assessment that claims certainty.

They do not need a system that tells them what they "should" become.

Everleap should resist the temptation to simplify people into categories.

---

# Emotional Goals

When a high school student uses Everleap, they should feel:

- Curious
- Understood
- Encouraged
- Hopeful
- Capable
- Comfortable exploring uncertainty

They should never feel:

- Judged
- Diagnosed
- Ranked
- Compared
- Boxed into an identity
- Pressured to decide too early

---

# How Everleap Should Speak

Language should be:

- Natural
- Warm
- Respectful
- Intelligent
- Honest
- Curious

Avoid:

- Corporate language
- Academic language
- Therapy language
- Motivational clichés
- Adult business terminology

Teenagers recognize authenticity quickly.

The product should always sound like a thoughtful mentor rather than an authority figure.

---

# Design Principles

High school students are still developing executive function.

The product should reduce cognitive load.

Every screen should have one clear purpose.

Every recommendation should feel achievable.

Progress should feel continuous rather than overwhelming.

The interface should encourage momentum through many small successes instead of a few large milestones.

---

# AI Guidelines

Artificial intelligence should act like an experienced mentor.

Not a teacher.

Not a parent.

Not a therapist.

Not an expert who already knows the answer.

Instead, AI should help students notice patterns they may have overlooked.

It should ask thoughtful questions.

It should acknowledge uncertainty.

It should encourage experimentation.

It should celebrate growth.

---

# Success

Everleap succeeds for high school students when they begin saying things like:

"I've never noticed that before."

"That actually sounds like me."

"I want to think about that."

"I want to try that."

"I understand myself a little better now."

Those reactions matter far more than producing perfect predictions.

The goal is not to tell students who they are.

The goal is to help them become increasingly confident in discovering that for themselves.

---

# The Standard

Whenever a product decision is made, ask:

> "Would this genuinely help a thoughtful sixteen-year-old better understand themselves?"

If the answer is yes, it is probably aligned with Everleap.

If the answer is no, it should be reconsidered.
