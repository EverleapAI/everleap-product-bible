# Young Adult Users

## Purpose

While Everleap is designed primarily for high school students, it should continue supporting users as they transition into adulthood.

Young adulthood is a period of rapid change.

Many assumptions made during high school are tested for the first time.

Students enter college.

Some begin careers.

Others change majors, transfer schools, take gap years, or discover entirely new interests.

Everleap should adapt to these changes rather than assuming earlier insights remain permanently true.

---

# Understanding the User

Young adults are no longer asking only:

"What should I do?"

Instead, they begin asking:

- Did I choose the right path?
- Why doesn't this feel the way I expected?
- Have I changed?
- What actually motivates me now?
- What kind of work gives me energy?
- What kind of life do I want?

These questions become increasingly complex because they are now informed by real experience rather than imagination.

Everleap should recognize this shift.

---

# Their Reality

Young adults experience significant transitions.

They often move away from home.

They build new friendships.

They experience greater independence.

Many encounter failure for the first time.

Others discover unexpected strengths.

Identity becomes less theoretical and more experiential.

Everleap should help users process these experiences rather than simply collecting more answers.

---

# What They Need

Young adults need perspective.

They need help recognizing how they have changed.

They need reminders that changing direction is normal.

They need confidence that discovering new interests does not invalidate earlier choices.

Most importantly, they need a product that evolves alongside them.

---

# What They Do NOT Need

They do not need to feel trapped by answers they gave years earlier.

They do not need the product insisting they are still the same person.

They do not need recommendations based solely on historical data.

Growth should always be allowed to change the story.

---

# Emotional Goals

Young adults should feel:

- More self-aware
- More confident
- More adaptable
- More intentional
- Less anxious about changing direction

The product should reduce fear of making mistakes.

Mistakes create evidence.

Evidence creates understanding.

---

# How Everleap Should Speak

Language should become slightly more mature than the high school experience while remaining conversational.

Avoid:

- Corporate career advice
- Self-help clichés
- Therapy language
- Executive coaching language

Everleap should sound like an experienced mentor who understands that adulthood is rarely a straight line.

---

# Design Principles

The experience should increasingly emphasize:

- Reflection
- Pattern recognition
- Longitudinal growth
- Comparing past and present
- Learning from experience

The product should begin showing users how they have changed over time.

Growth should become visible.

---

# AI Guidelines

Artificial intelligence should begin making deeper connections across months and years.

It should help users notice:

- recurring themes
- changing motivations
- developing strengths
- evolving goals
- lessons learned

AI should never imply that changing direction represents failure.

Instead, it should recognize adaptation as evidence of growth.

---

# Success

Everleap succeeds for young adults when users begin saying:

"I've changed more than I realized."

"I can see why that decision made sense at the time."

"I understand what motivates me much better now."

"I feel more confident making my next decision."

The goal is not to eliminate uncertainty.

The goal is to help users become increasingly comfortable navigating it.

---

# The Standard

Whenever a product decision affects young adults, ask:

> "Does this help someone better understand how they are evolving rather than who they were?"

If the answer is yes, the experience is aligned with Everleap's purpose.
