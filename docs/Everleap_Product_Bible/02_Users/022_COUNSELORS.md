# Counselors

## Purpose

Counselors are one of Everleap's most important audiences.

Everleap is not designed to replace counselors.

It is designed to make counselors more effective.

The product should provide counselors with richer evidence, better questions, and clearer developmental patterns so that every conversation with a student becomes more meaningful.

---

# Understanding the User

School counselors face an impossible challenge.

They are responsible for supporting hundreds, sometimes thousands, of students.

Most have very little time with each individual.

Important conversations are often compressed into thirty-minute meetings.

Many students struggle to explain what they are feeling or what they are interested in.

Everleap exists to make those conversations easier.

---

# The Counselor's Goal

Counselors rarely need another assessment.

They need better understanding.

Their goal is to help students make thoughtful decisions.

They want to know:

- What motivates this student?
- What environments help them succeed?
- What strengths seem to be developing?
- What questions should we explore together?
- Where does this student need encouragement?

Everleap should provide evidence that supports those conversations.

---

# Everleap's Role

Everleap should act as preparation.

Students arrive having already reflected.

Patterns have already begun to emerge.

Questions have already been collected.

This allows counselors to spend less time gathering information and more time discussing what matters.

The best counseling conversations begin where Everleap ends.

---

# What Counselors Should Never Receive

Everleap should never present itself as having diagnosed a student.

It should never assign personality labels.

It should never recommend a single career path.

It should never imply certainty about a student's future.

Counselors should always receive observations, evidence, and hypotheses rather than conclusions.

---

# Explainability

Every meaningful observation should be explainable.

If a counselor asks:

"Why did Everleap make this observation?"

There should be supporting evidence.

This evidence may include:

- Story responses
- Reflection history
- Science observations
- Longitudinal patterns
- User actions

Trust depends upon transparency.

---

# Professional Judgment

Counselors always remain the experts.

Everleap contributes another perspective.

The product should encourage counselors to question, validate, expand, or reject its observations.

Healthy disagreement improves understanding.

---

# Supporting Conversations

Everleap should help counselors ask better questions.

Examples include:

- What experiences have given you the most energy recently?
- Has this pattern always been true?
- Where does this observation not feel accurate?
- What surprised you most about these insights?
- What would you like to explore next?

The goal is conversation.

Not confirmation.

---

# Longitudinal Growth

One advantage Everleap provides is memory.

The system can recognize changes over months or years.

Counselors should be able to see:

- changing motivations
- growing confidence
- emerging strengths
- evolving interests
- completed actions
- shifts in priorities

Growth should become visible.

---

# Success

Everleap succeeds when counselors begin saying:

"This helped me understand the student before we even started talking."

"It gave us a much better place to begin."

"It helped the student express something they hadn't been able to explain."

The goal is not to replace counseling.

The goal is to improve every counseling conversation.

---

# The Standard

Whenever a feature affects counselors, ask:

> "Does this help a counselor have a deeper, more productive conversation with a student?"

If the answer is yes, it supports Everleap's mission.

If the answer is no, it should be reconsidered.
