# Parents

## Purpose

Parents are important partners in a young person's development.

Everleap is not designed to replace parental guidance.

It is designed to improve the conversations parents have with their children.

The product should help parents become more curious, more supportive, and less focused on immediate answers.

---

# Understanding the User

Parents want the best for their children.

They want them to succeed.

They want them to be happy.

They want them to make good decisions.

Unfortunately, these goals often create pressure.

Questions like:

- What college are you going to?
- What career do you want?
- What are you going to major in?

are usually asked with good intentions.

But they can unintentionally communicate that having answers is more important than understanding oneself.

Everleap should gently shift that conversation.

---

# The Parent's Challenge

Parents know their children well.

But they often know them through the lens of family.

Teachers see another side.

Friends see another side.

Coaches see another side.

Students themselves experience thoughts and motivations that parents may never see.

Everleap helps bring these different perspectives together.

---

# Everleap's Role

Everleap should encourage parents to become partners in discovery.

Instead of asking:

"What are you going to do?"

Parents may begin asking:

- What have you learned about yourself?
- What surprised you?
- Which insights felt accurate?
- Which ones didn't?
- What would you like to explore next?

These conversations create understanding rather than pressure.

---

# What Parents Should Never Believe

Everleap should never imply that it understands a child better than their family does.

It should never suggest that an assessment defines a child's future.

It should never encourage parents to use insights as labels.

Instead, the product should remind parents that every observation is a hypothesis supported by current evidence.

Growth remains possible.

---

# Encouraging Healthy Conversations

The best parent conversations are collaborative.

Everleap should encourage questions rather than conclusions.

Examples include:

- What part of this feels most like you?
- What part doesn't fit?
- Have you always been this way?
- What do you think has changed?
- What would you like to learn more about?

The goal is understanding.

Not agreement.

---

# Supporting Independence

One of Everleap's responsibilities is helping young people become increasingly independent.

Parents should gradually shift from directing decisions to supporting thoughtful decision-making.

Everleap should strengthen that transition.

The product should help parents become coaches rather than decision makers.

---

# Building Trust

Parents should trust Everleap because it is:

- evidence-based
- transparent
- humble
- explainable
- respectful of uncertainty

Trust should never depend upon sounding certain.

Trust grows when the product openly acknowledges what it knows and what it does not yet know.

---

# Success

Everleap succeeds for parents when conversations begin changing.

Instead of asking:

"What career should you choose?"

Parents begin asking:

"What kind of work seems to give you energy?"

Instead of focusing on outcomes...

they begin exploring motivations.

Instead of expecting certainty...

they become comfortable with growth.

---

# The Standard

Whenever a feature affects parents, ask:

> "Will this help parents better understand and support their child's development without reducing their independence?"

If the answer is yes, it aligns with Everleap's mission.

If the answer is no, it should be redesigned.
