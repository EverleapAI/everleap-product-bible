# Story

## Purpose

Story is the evidence engine of Everleap.

If Today is the coach...

and Insights is the explanation...

Story is where understanding begins.

Story exists to collect meaningful evidence about a person's developing motivations, strengths, skills, values, preferences, and experiences.

Unlike traditional assessments, Story is not trying to produce a score.

It is trying to build understanding.

---

# The Question Story Answers

Story answers one question:

> **"What evidence helps us better understand this person?"**

Not:

"Who is this person?"

That distinction defines the entire experience.

---

# Philosophy

People reveal themselves through many small observations.

One answer means very little.

Twenty answers begin to suggest patterns.

Fifty answers begin to reveal consistency.

Story should never rush toward conclusions.

Its responsibility is collecting evidence, not interpreting it.

Interpretation belongs to the sciences.

---

# A Conversation, Not A Test

Story should never feel like taking an assessment.

There are:

- no scores
- no right answers
- no wrong answers
- no passing
- no failing

Instead, Story should feel like a thoughtful conversation.

Each question should make the user think.

Not perform.

---

# Every Question Must Have A Purpose

Questions should never exist because they are interesting.

Every question must contribute evidence toward one or more scientific models.

If a question cannot be justified, it should not exist.

Every answer should make the product slightly smarter.

---

# Progressive Discovery

Story is intentionally progressive.

The user is never asked everything at once.

Instead:

small conversations

become

larger understanding.

This reduces fatigue while improving answer quality.

---

# Scientific Foundation

Story itself contains no science.

It simply gathers evidence.

That evidence is later interpreted independently through multiple scientific lenses.

Current sciences include:

- Ikigai
- Enneagram
- What Color Is Your Parachute

Future sciences may include additional behavioral frameworks.

Story should remain independent of any one scientific model.

---

# Question Design

Questions should be:

- clear
- conversational
- reflective
- emotionally safe
- age appropriate

Questions should avoid:

- jargon
- psychology terminology
- academic wording
- obvious "correct" answers

Users should answer naturally rather than strategically.

---

# Progress

Progress should feel encouraging.

Never overwhelming.

Users should always know they are moving forward.

But they should never feel pressured to finish.

Story is not something to complete.

It is something to continue.

---

# Relationship To Insights

Story never tells users what their answers mean.

Insights exist for that purpose.

Story collects.

Insights explain.

Keeping these responsibilities separate creates trust.

---

# Relationship To Today

Story creates evidence.

Today uses evidence.

The more Story grows...

the more personal Today becomes.

Every Story answer should improve future coaching.

---

# AI Responsibilities

During Story, AI acts primarily as an interviewer.

Its responsibilities include:

- asking thoughtful questions
- keeping conversations natural
- clarifying when necessary
- maintaining engagement

It should not begin interpreting answers.

That comes later.

---

# Success

Story succeeds when users begin forgetting they are answering questions.

Instead they begin reflecting.

Thinking.

Remembering.

Wondering.

That shift—from answering to reflecting—is where meaningful evidence begins.

---

# Anti-Patterns

Story should never:

- become repetitive
- ask unnecessary questions
- explain psychology
- provide insights prematurely
- reward completion over reflection
- become a personality quiz

---

# Product Rules

Story exists to collect evidence.

Nothing more.

Nothing less.

Every question should increase understanding.

Every answer should become another piece of a much larger picture.

Understanding is built gradually.

Story is where that process begins.
