# Motivations

## Purpose

The Motivations page helps users understand **why** certain experiences, activities, and environments consistently give them energy and meaning.

This page is primarily informed by the Ikigai science model.

Its purpose is not to explain what a user is good at.

It is not to recommend careers.

It is not to predict success.

Its responsibility is to help users better understand what appears to matter most to them.

---

# The Question This Page Answers

Every page answers one question.

Motivations answers:

> **"What seems to give my life energy and meaning?"**

Everything on this page should contribute to answering that question.

Nothing else.

---

# Philosophy

Motivation is deeper than interest.

People often enjoy many things.

Far fewer things create sustained energy.

Motivations explain why someone continues showing up even when something becomes difficult.

Understanding motivation helps explain many later decisions.

It influences:

- persistence
- engagement
- fulfillment
- satisfaction
- resilience

Understanding motivation is one of the foundations of intentional living.

---

# Scientific Foundation

This page is primarily driven by the Ikigai science.

Rather than looking at isolated answers, Ikigai searches for recurring intersections between:

- what energizes the user
- what they naturally enjoy
- where they may contribute to others
- what feels meaningful
- what strengths repeatedly appear

The page should present emerging hypotheses rather than conclusions.

---

# What This Page Contains

The page should help users understand:

- recurring motivational themes
- situations that consistently create energy
- conditions where motivation increases
- conditions where motivation decreases
- competing motivations
- unanswered motivational questions

The page should encourage exploration rather than agreement.

---

# What This Page Should Never Do

This page should never:

- recommend careers
- assign identity
- diagnose personality
- flatter the user
- summarize biography

It should remain focused on motivations.

---

# Evidence

Every observation should be traceable to evidence.

Evidence may include:

- Story responses
- repeated themes
- science memo observations
- completed actions
- historical patterns

If evidence is weak, the page should acknowledge uncertainty.

---

# Confidence

Confidence reflects the amount of supporting evidence.

Lower confidence should encourage additional exploration.

Higher confidence allows observations to become more specific.

Confidence never becomes certainty.

---

# Relationship To Other Pages

Motivations explains why.

Strengths explains how.

Skills explains what.

Together they create a richer understanding than any page alone.

The pages should complement one another rather than overlap.

---

# AI Responsibilities

The AI responsible for Motivations should:

- identify recurring motivational patterns
- explain why those patterns matter
- highlight uncertainty
- encourage future exploration

It should never:

- predict careers
- tell users who they are
- repeat Story answers
- invent evidence

---

# Writing Style

Writing should feel:

- thoughtful
- curious
- evidence-based
- hopeful
- intellectually honest

The page should sound like someone helping the user notice patterns they may have overlooked.

---

# Success

Motivations succeeds when users think:

"I understand why certain experiences affect me."

"I've never connected those ideas before."

"That explains why some things feel so rewarding."

The goal is understanding.

Not certainty.

---

# Anti-Patterns

Avoid:

- career advice
- personality labels
- generic inspiration
- exaggerated confidence
- unsupported observations
- repeating Story responses

---

# Product Rules

Every observation should answer:

1. What motivation is emerging?
2. What evidence supports it?
3. Why might it matter?

If those questions cannot be answered, the observation should not appear.

The page exists to deepen self-understanding—not to define identity.
