# Ikigai

## Purpose

Ikigai is Everleap's primary science for understanding motivation.

It does not attempt to predict careers.

It does not identify purpose.

It does not tell users they have "found their Ikigai."

Instead, it helps answer one fundamental question:

> **"What appears to give this person energy, meaning, and a sense of contribution?"**

Ikigai is the science that helps Everleap understand **why** someone continues showing up for certain experiences while walking away from others.

---

# Why Ikigai

Most career tools begin with occupations.

Ikigai begins with people.

Everleap believes meaningful careers emerge from understanding motivations rather than selecting professions.

Understanding motivation creates better decisions throughout life—not just career decisions.

---

# The Question Ikigai Answers

Ikigai answers one question:

> **"What seems most meaningful to this person?"**

Everything produced by the Ikigai science should contribute to answering that question.

Nothing else.

---

# The Five Dimensions We Measure

Everleap adapts the traditional Ikigai framework for developmental coaching.

Rather than treating the famous diagram as a career exercise, we use it as a model for understanding motivation across five intersecting dimensions.

## 1. Energy

What consistently energizes the user?

Examples include:

- solving problems
- helping people
- building things
- teaching
- creating
- leading
- discovering
- exploring

Energy is often visible long before purpose.

---

## 2. Enjoyment

What activities does the user naturally enjoy repeating?

Interest alone is weak evidence.

Repeated enjoyment across different situations is much stronger evidence.

---

## 3. Strength

Where do natural strengths repeatedly appear?

Ikigai does not identify strengths itself.

Instead it recognizes where motivations consistently align with demonstrated capability.

Strength without motivation rarely creates fulfillment.

Motivation without strength often creates frustration.

The intersection matters.

---

## 4. Contribution

How does the user appear to enjoy contributing to other people?

Contribution is broader than employment.

Examples include:

- teaching
- organizing
- encouraging
- protecting
- creating
- solving
- supporting
- leading

Contribution helps transform interests into meaning.

---

## 5. Future Potential

Rather than asking:

"What career fits?"

Everleap asks:

"What future opportunities might naturally emerge if these motivational patterns continue developing?"

Future potential is exploratory.

Never predictive.

---

# The Intersections

The value of Ikigai comes from intersections.

A single observation rarely matters.

Multiple dimensions aligning create meaningful hypotheses.

For example:

High Energy

+

High Enjoyment

+

Strong Contribution

may indicate an experience worth exploring further.

Likewise,

Strong Ability

without

Enjoyment

may explain why someone succeeds but feels unfulfilled.

The intersections matter more than any single circle.

---

# Evidence

Ikigai draws evidence from:

- Story responses
- repeated themes
- actions completed
- longitudinal changes
- reflection history
- future behavioral evidence

One answer should never drive an Ikigai conclusion.

Patterns matter.

---

# What Ikigai Produces

The Ikigai science produces structured internal observations including:

- motivational hypotheses
- competing explanations
- confidence
- supporting evidence
- unanswered questions
- missing information

These outputs become science memos.

Science memos are not user-facing.

---

# Confidence

Confidence reflects convergence.

When multiple independent observations support the same motivational hypothesis, confidence increases.

When evidence conflicts, confidence decreases.

Low confidence should produce more questions.

Not weaker science.

---

# Relationship To Other Sciences

Ikigai answers:

**Why does this seem meaningful?**

Enneagram answers:

**How does this person naturally approach life?**

What Color Is Your Parachute answers:

**What capabilities consistently appear?**

Each science contributes one perspective.

The Summary later combines them.

---

# AI Responsibilities

The Ikigai scientist should:

- search for motivational patterns
- recognize meaningful intersections
- identify competing explanations
- acknowledge uncertainty
- recommend future questions

It should never:

- predict careers
- assign purpose
- claim the user has found their Ikigai
- flatten motivation into personality

Its responsibility is understanding motivation.

Nothing more.

---

# Success

Ikigai succeeds when users begin thinking:

"I understand why some experiences matter so much to me."

"I can see what keeps pulling me back."

"I understand what gives me energy."

Those insights create the foundation for future exploration.

---

# Anti-Patterns

Ikigai should never become:

- a career quiz
- a personality assessment
- a purpose generator
- a strengths report
- a list of interests

Its responsibility is motivation.

Everything else belongs elsewhere.

---

# Product Rules

Every Ikigai hypothesis should answer:

1. What motivational pattern is emerging?
2. What evidence supports it?
3. Which intersections strengthen this hypothesis?
4. What competing explanations still exist?
5. What additional evidence would increase confidence?

Ikigai should always leave room for growth.

Purpose is discovered through living.

Not predicted through assessment.
