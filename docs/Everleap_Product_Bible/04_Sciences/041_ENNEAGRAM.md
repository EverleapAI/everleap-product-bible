# Enneagram

## Purpose

The Enneagram is Everleap's primary science for understanding recurring motivational and behavioral patterns.

It is **not** used as a personality typing system.

Everleap does not tell users they are a Type 1, Type 7, or any other Enneagram type.

Instead, the Enneagram provides a framework for asking:

> **"What internal motivations appear to consistently shape this person's behavior?"**

The science helps explain *how* a person naturally approaches challenges, relationships, achievement, responsibility, uncertainty, and growth.

---

# Why Enneagram

Most personality systems describe observable behavior.

The Enneagram attempts to explain the motivations beneath that behavior.

Two people may behave identically for completely different reasons.

For example:

Both may volunteer to lead.

One may lead because they enjoy responsibility.

Another may lead because uncertainty makes them uncomfortable.

The behavior is identical.

The motivation is different.

Understanding motivation creates better self-awareness than describing behavior alone.

---

# The Question Enneagram Answers

Enneagram answers one question:

> **"What internal motivations most consistently explain this person's behavior?"**

Everything produced by this science should contribute to answering that question.

Nothing else.

---

# What We Measure

Everleap uses the Enneagram to observe recurring motivational patterns across several dimensions.

## Core Motivations

What repeatedly drives this person's choices?

Examples include:

- mastery
- achievement
- security
- contribution
- autonomy
- harmony
- belonging
- exploration
- responsibility

These are hypotheses.

Not identities.

---

## Internal Drivers

What appears to create momentum?

Examples include:

- challenge
- helping others
- curiosity
- solving problems
- improving systems
- recognition
- learning
- independence

Internal drivers often remain surprisingly stable over time.

---

## Adaptive Strengths

Many strengths begin as adaptive strategies.

Examples include:

- organization
- empathy
- perseverance
- courage
- creativity
- leadership
- optimism
- precision

The Enneagram helps identify why these strengths appear.

---

## Growth Patterns

People respond differently as confidence grows.

Some become more adventurous.

Others become more collaborative.

Others become more reflective.

Everleap should recognize these patterns over time.

Growth is evidence.

---

## Stress Patterns

Behavior changes under pressure.

The purpose is **not** to diagnose.

The purpose is to recognize recurring tendencies.

Examples include:

- becoming more controlling
- withdrawing
- seeking reassurance
- taking excessive responsibility
- avoiding conflict
- becoming overly independent

These observations should always remain tentative.

---

# What Enneagram Produces

The Enneagram science produces structured observations including:

- motivational hypotheses
- behavioral patterns
- adaptive strengths
- growth opportunities
- competing explanations
- supporting evidence
- confidence

These outputs become internal science memos.

They are not shown directly to users.

---

# Evidence

Enneagram uses evidence from:

- Story responses
- longitudinal patterns
- repeated behaviors
- completed actions
- future reflections
- future coaching interactions

Single answers should never determine conclusions.

Recurring evidence matters.

---

# Confidence

Confidence increases when multiple independent observations support the same motivational hypothesis.

Confidence decreases when competing explanations remain equally plausible.

Low confidence should generate better future questions.

Not weaker science.

---

# Relationship To Other Sciences

Ikigai explains:

**Why does this matter?**

Enneagram explains:

**What internal motivations repeatedly shape behavior?**

What Color Is Your Parachute explains:

**What capabilities naturally emerge?**

Together they create a richer understanding than any science alone.

---

# AI Responsibilities

The Enneagram scientist should:

- recognize recurring motivational patterns
- identify internal drivers
- acknowledge competing explanations
- distinguish behavior from motivation
- recommend future questions that reduce uncertainty

It should never:

- assign Enneagram types
- diagnose personality
- label users
- present certainty where uncertainty exists

Its job is understanding motivation beneath behavior.

Nothing more.

---

# Success

The Enneagram succeeds when users begin thinking:

"I understand why I react that way."

"That explains something I've always noticed."

"I never realized that motivation was driving so many decisions."

Those realizations increase self-awareness without reducing identity to a label.

---

# Anti-Patterns

The Enneagram should never become:

- a personality quiz
- a typing system
- a strengths report
- a career assessment
- a diagnosis

It exists to understand recurring internal motivations.

Not to define the person.

---

# Product Rules

Every Enneagram hypothesis should answer:

1. What recurring internal motivation appears to be emerging?
2. What evidence supports it?
3. What competing explanations still exist?
4. How might this motivation influence behavior?
5. What future evidence would strengthen or weaken this hypothesis?

The Enneagram should always produce better questions than answers.

Self-awareness grows through reflection, not categorization.
