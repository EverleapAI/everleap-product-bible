# Self-Determination Theory (SDT)

> **Status: planned — not yet implemented.** SDT is not registered in
> `scienceRegistry.ts`. The implemented sciences today are Ikigai, Enneagram,
> and What Color Is Your Parachute (Skills). This document is the design intent
> for when SDT is added.

## Purpose

Self-Determination Theory (SDT) helps Everleap understand **what conditions allow a person to thrive**.

Unlike Ikigai, which explores meaning...

or Enneagram, which explores recurring motivations...

or WCIYP, which explores transferable capabilities...

SDT explains **why motivation increases or decreases in different environments.**

It answers one simple question:

> **"What conditions help this person become their best?"**

---

# Why SDT

People often assume motivation is a personality trait.

It isn't.

Motivation changes dramatically depending on the environment.

A student who appears lazy in one classroom may become highly motivated in another.

A person who dislikes one job may become deeply engaged in another.

The difference is often the environment.

Understanding those conditions helps users intentionally seek situations where they are most likely to flourish.

---

# The Question SDT Answers

Self-Determination Theory answers:

> **"What environments naturally increase this person's motivation?"**

Everything produced by SDT should contribute to answering that question.

Nothing else.

---

# The Three Psychological Needs

SDT proposes that human motivation is strongest when three basic psychological needs are supported.

Everleap uses these dimensions as developmental hypotheses rather than fixed measurements.

---

## Autonomy

Autonomy asks:

> "How important is having choice?"

Some people become energized when given freedom.

Others prefer structure.

Neither is better.

The science simply attempts to understand which conditions consistently produce greater engagement.

Evidence may include:

- preference for independence
- enjoyment of creating personal approaches
- reactions to rules
- responses to structured environments

---

## Competence

Competence asks:

> "When does this person feel capable?"

People naturally seek opportunities to become more effective.

Some enjoy mastering difficult skills.

Others enjoy applying existing strengths.

Everleap looks for evidence of:

- persistence
- learning
- skill development
- enjoyment of challenge
- confidence growth

The goal is not measuring ability.

It is understanding what creates confidence.

---

## Relatedness

Relatedness asks:

> "When does connection matter?"

Some people become energized through collaboration.

Others through mentoring.

Others through belonging to meaningful communities.

Everleap looks for evidence of:

- teamwork
- relationships
- contribution
- community
- belonging
- shared purpose

Connection often influences motivation more than ability.

---

# What SDT Produces

The science produces hypotheses about:

- autonomy preferences
- competence needs
- relatedness needs
- motivational environments
- conditions that increase engagement
- conditions that reduce engagement
- supporting evidence
- confidence

These observations become internal science memos.

---

# Evidence

SDT uses evidence from:

- Story responses
- repeated choices
- completed actions
- longitudinal observations
- motivational patterns
- future behavioral evidence

No single answer determines an SDT observation.

Patterns across time matter.

---

# Confidence

Confidence increases when multiple observations consistently point toward the same environmental preferences.

Confidence decreases when evidence conflicts.

Low confidence should produce additional questions.

Not stronger claims.

---

# Relationship To Other Sciences

Ikigai answers:

**Why does this matter?**

Enneagram answers:

**What internal motivations shape behavior?**

WCIYP answers:

**What capabilities naturally emerge?**

SDT answers:

**What environments allow those motivations and capabilities to flourish?**

Together they provide a richer understanding of human development.

---

# AI Responsibilities

The SDT scientist should:

- recognize environmental patterns
- identify motivational conditions
- distinguish between ability and environment
- acknowledge uncertainty
- recommend future questions

It should never:

- diagnose motivation
- imply laziness
- judge performance
- recommend careers directly

Its responsibility is understanding the environments where motivation naturally grows.

---

# Success

SDT succeeds when users begin thinking:

"I understand why I do better in some situations than others."

"I can see what kinds of environments help me succeed."

"I know what I should look for when choosing future opportunities."

Those insights help users intentionally build environments where they can thrive.

---

# Anti-Patterns

SDT should never become:

- a productivity score
- a work ethic assessment
- a personality profile
- a performance evaluation

Its purpose is understanding motivational environments.

Nothing more.

---

# Product Rules

Every SDT hypothesis should answer:

1. What environmental condition appears important?
2. What evidence supports it?
3. Which of the three psychological needs does it strengthen?
4. What competing explanations still exist?
5. What future evidence would improve confidence?

Self-Determination Theory helps Everleap understand not just who a person is, but where they are most likely to become their best.
