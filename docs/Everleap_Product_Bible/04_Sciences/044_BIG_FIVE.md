# Big Five (Five-Factor Model)

> **Status: planned — not yet implemented.** The Big Five is not registered in
> `scienceRegistry.ts`. The implemented sciences today are Ikigai, Enneagram,
> and What Color Is Your Parachute (Skills). This document is the design intent
> for when the Big Five is added.

## Purpose

The Big Five provides Everleap with a framework for understanding broad behavioral tendencies.

Unlike many personality assessments, the Big Five does not attempt to explain *why* people behave the way they do.

Instead, it describes **how behavior tends to vary across five well-researched dimensions.**

Within Everleap, the Big Five is used only as a supporting science.

It never defines the user.

It contributes one additional perspective to the overall understanding.

---

# Why Big Five

The Five-Factor Model is one of the most extensively researched models in personality psychology.

Its value comes from describing observable behavioral tendencies rather than assigning personality types.

Everleap uses this science because it helps explain recurring patterns without reducing people to categories.

It complements the other sciences rather than replacing them.

---

# The Question Big Five Answers

The Big Five answers:

> **"What broad behavioral tendencies appear consistently across many situations?"**

It does not answer:

- Who is this person?
- What career should they choose?
- What motivates them?

Those questions belong to other sciences.

---

# The Five Dimensions

Everleap considers all five dimensions as continuous spectrums.

People are never placed into fixed categories.

---

## Openness

Explores a person's willingness to:

- explore ideas
- imagine possibilities
- try new experiences
- appreciate creativity
- enjoy abstract thinking

High openness may appear as curiosity.

Lower openness may appear as a preference for familiarity and practicality.

Neither is inherently better.

---

## Conscientiousness

Explores tendencies related to:

- organization
- planning
- reliability
- persistence
- self-discipline

Everleap does not treat conscientiousness as a measure of character.

It is simply one way people naturally differ.

---

## Extraversion

Explores where people often gain energy.

Examples include:

- interacting with others
- leading discussions
- seeking stimulation
- preferring quiet reflection
- enjoying independent work

This dimension is about preferred energy sources rather than social ability.

---

## Agreeableness

Explores tendencies toward:

- cooperation
- empathy
- trust
- compassion
- collaboration

It does not measure kindness.

It simply observes recurring interpersonal patterns.

---

## Emotional Stability

Traditionally referred to as Neuroticism.

Everleap intentionally frames this dimension positively.

The science explores how people tend to respond emotionally to:

- uncertainty
- stress
- change
- setbacks
- pressure

This is not a mental health assessment.

It is one small piece of understanding human variation.

---

# What Big Five Produces

The science produces hypotheses about:

- behavioral tendencies
- recurring patterns
- consistency across situations
- confidence
- supporting evidence
- unanswered questions

These observations become internal science memos.

They are not shown directly to users.

---

# Evidence

Big Five draws evidence from:

- Story responses
- repeated behavioral choices
- completed actions
- long-term observations
- future behavioral evidence

The science values consistency across many observations.

One answer should never influence an entire dimension.

---

# Confidence

Confidence increases when behavioral evidence remains consistent over time.

Confidence decreases when behavior changes significantly across situations.

Behavior is contextual.

Everleap should acknowledge that.

---

# Relationship To Other Sciences

Ikigai explains:

**Why something matters.**

Enneagram explains:

**What motivations may drive behavior.**

WCIYP explains:

**What capabilities naturally emerge.**

SDT explains:

**What environments increase motivation.**

Big Five explains:

**What broad behavioral tendencies repeatedly appear.**

Each science contributes one perspective.

None defines the whole person.

---

# AI Responsibilities

The Big Five scientist should:

- recognize recurring behavioral tendencies
- distinguish patterns from isolated events
- acknowledge uncertainty
- avoid stereotypes
- recommend future observations

It should never:

- assign personality labels
- diagnose mental health
- compare users
- predict success
- imply fixed identity

Its responsibility is describing recurring behavioral tendencies.

Nothing more.

---

# Success

Big Five succeeds when users begin thinking:

"I've noticed that about myself."

"I can see that pattern."

"That helps explain why certain situations feel more natural."

The science should increase awareness.

Not create identity.

---

# Anti-Patterns

Big Five should never become:

- a personality test
- a ranking system
- a mental health assessment
- a prediction engine
- an identity label

Behavior changes.

People grow.

The science should always leave room for development.

---

# Product Rules

Every Big Five hypothesis should answer:

1. What behavioral tendency appears to be emerging?
2. What evidence supports it?
3. How consistent is the pattern?
4. What situations strengthen or weaken it?
5. What additional evidence would improve confidence?

The Big Five should help Everleap describe behavior without pretending behavior fully explains the person.
