# Narrative Identity

## Purpose

Narrative Identity helps Everleap understand **how users make sense of their lives through the stories they tell about themselves.**

Unlike the other sciences, Narrative Identity is not primarily concerned with motivations, skills, or behavioral tendencies.

It is concerned with meaning.

Specifically:

> **How does this person explain who they are becoming?**

The stories people tell about themselves influence the decisions they make, the risks they take, and the futures they imagine.

Understanding those stories allows Everleap to support healthier, more intentional self-development.

---

# Why Narrative Identity

People do not experience life as isolated events.

They organize experiences into stories.

Examples include:

"I've always been the responsible one."

"I'm not creative."

"I've never been good at making friends."

"I love solving impossible problems."

These stories become identities.

Some help people grow.

Some quietly limit them.

Everleap should recognize these narratives without reinforcing them unnecessarily.

---

# The Question Narrative Identity Answers

Narrative Identity answers:

> **"What story does this person appear to be telling themselves about who they are becoming?"**

This is not the same as asking who they are.

It asks how they currently understand themselves.

Stories can change.

That is the opportunity.

---

# The Stories We Observe

Narrative Identity looks for recurring themes such as:

- resilience
- exploration
- responsibility
- independence
- curiosity
- contribution
- leadership
- creativity
- perseverance
- belonging

These are narrative themes.

Not personality traits.

---

# Growth Narratives

Some stories encourage growth.

Examples include:

"I can learn almost anything."

"I usually figure things out."

"I enjoy difficult challenges."

These stories often increase confidence and resilience.

Everleap should reinforce evidence that supports healthy developmental narratives.

---

# Limiting Narratives

Some stories quietly reduce possibility.

Examples include:

"I'm just not a math person."

"I'm terrible with people."

"I always quit."

"I'm not leadership material."

Everleap should never directly argue against these beliefs.

Instead, it should introduce evidence that encourages users to reconsider them.

Growth is more persuasive than contradiction.

---

# Turning Points

Certain experiences reshape identity.

Examples include:

- overcoming failure
- discovering a new interest
- helping another person
- achieving something difficult
- changing schools
- joining a community

Narrative Identity should recognize when important turning points appear.

These moments often reshape future decisions.

---

# What Narrative Identity Produces

The science produces hypotheses about:

- recurring life themes
- emerging personal narratives
- growth stories
- limiting stories
- turning points
- supporting evidence
- competing interpretations
- confidence

These become internal science memos.

They are not shown directly to users.

---

# Evidence

Narrative Identity uses evidence from:

- Story responses
- reflections
- user language
- completed actions
- longitudinal changes
- future conversations

Language matters.

The words users choose often reveal the stories they believe.

---

# Confidence

Confidence increases when similar narratives appear across multiple conversations over time.

Confidence decreases when narratives frequently change.

Changing narratives are not errors.

They are evidence of growth.

---

# Relationship To Other Sciences

Ikigai explains:

**What gives life meaning.**

Enneagram explains:

**What motivations shape behavior.**

WCIYP explains:

**What capabilities naturally emerge.**

SDT explains:

**What environments help motivation grow.**

Big Five explains:

**What behavioral tendencies appear.**

Narrative Identity explains:

**How the user currently understands their own story.**

It connects the other sciences into a personal narrative.

---

# AI Responsibilities

The Narrative Identity scientist should:

- recognize recurring stories
- identify turning points
- notice changes in self-description
- distinguish evidence from interpretation
- encourage healthier narratives without replacing the user's voice

It should never:

- rewrite someone's life
- invent turning points
- exaggerate meaning
- diagnose trauma
- replace professional support

Its responsibility is understanding the stories users tell about themselves.

Nothing more.

---

# Success

Narrative Identity succeeds when users begin thinking:

"I never realized I keep telling that story."

"Maybe that's not the whole picture."

"I've changed more than I thought."

"I think I'm becoming someone different."

These moments often produce the deepest personal growth.

---

# Anti-Patterns

Narrative Identity should never become:

- therapy
- autobiography
- journaling
- diagnosis
- life coaching

It exists to recognize patterns in personal meaning-making.

Not to replace the user's own voice.

---

# Product Rules

Every Narrative Identity hypothesis should answer:

1. What story appears to be emerging?
2. What evidence supports that story?
3. What alternative stories also fit the evidence?
4. Has this story changed over time?
5. How might future experiences reshape it?

Narrative Identity reminds Everleap that people do not simply accumulate experiences.

They create meaning from them.

Helping users examine those meanings is one of the most powerful ways Everleap can support lifelong growth.
