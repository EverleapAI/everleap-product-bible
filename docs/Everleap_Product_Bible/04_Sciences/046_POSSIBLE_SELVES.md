# Possible Selves

## Purpose

Possible Selves helps Everleap understand **who the user may become if they continue growing in different directions.**

Unlike the other sciences, Possible Selves is intentionally future-oriented.

It is not concerned with predicting the future.

It is concerned with helping users imagine multiple futures that are both realistic and personally meaningful.

It answers one question:

> **"Who could this person become?"**

Not:

> "Who will they become?"

That distinction is fundamental.

---

# Why Possible Selves

Research shows that people are strongly influenced by the futures they imagine.

People naturally develop mental pictures of:

- who they hope to become
- who they expect to become
- who they fear becoming

These imagined futures influence:

- motivation
- persistence
- confidence
- resilience
- decision making

Everleap helps users explore these possible futures intentionally.

---

# The Question Possible Selves Answers

Possible Selves answers:

> **"What future versions of this person appear both possible and worth exploring?"**

The emphasis is on possibilities.

Not predictions.

Not certainty.

Not destiny.

---

# Multiple Futures

Everleap assumes there is never a single correct future.

Every user has many possible paths.

Examples might include becoming:

- a creator
- a leader
- a teacher
- an entrepreneur
- a researcher
- a designer
- an advocate
- an engineer
- an artist

These are examples of future identities.

Not occupations.

A person may express the same identity through many different careers.

---

# Hopeful Selves

Hopeful Selves represent futures the user genuinely wants.

These futures should emerge naturally from evidence.

They should feel:

- exciting
- meaningful
- believable
- motivating

Hopeful Selves should inspire action rather than fantasy.

---

# Expected Selves

Sometimes users describe futures they believe are likely.

These expectations may or may not match their hopes.

Everleap should notice the difference.

Understanding that gap often creates important conversations.

---

# Feared Selves

People are also motivated by futures they wish to avoid.

Examples include:

- feeling stuck
- wasting potential
- disappointing others
- lacking purpose
- losing independence

Everleap should recognize these concerns carefully.

The goal is not fear.

The goal is understanding what motivates action.

---

# Identity Experiments

Possible Selves should encourage experimentation.

Instead of asking:

"Is this who I am?"

The product should encourage:

"What would I learn if I explored this version of myself?"

Small experiments create better evidence than speculation.

---

# What Possible Selves Produces

The science produces hypotheses about:

- promising future identities
- motivating future possibilities
- conflicting future visions
- supporting evidence
- uncertainty
- confidence

These observations become internal science memos.

They are not presented directly to users.

---

# Evidence

Possible Selves uses evidence from:

- Story responses
- motivations
- strengths
- transferable skills
- completed actions
- exploration history
- changing interests
- longitudinal growth

The strongest future possibilities emerge when multiple sciences point toward similar directions.

---

# Confidence

Confidence reflects the amount of supporting evidence.

Higher confidence means:

"This future appears increasingly consistent with observed patterns."

It never means:

"This future will happen."

Possible Selves should always leave room for change.

---

# Relationship To Other Sciences

Ikigai explains:

**Why something matters.**

Enneagram explains:

**What motivations repeatedly shape behavior.**

WCIYP explains:

**What capabilities naturally emerge.**

SDT explains:

**Where motivation grows.**

Big Five explains:

**What behavioral tendencies appear.**

Narrative Identity explains:

**How the user understands their story.**

Possible Selves asks:

**"Given everything we know, what futures seem worth exploring?"**

It is the most future-oriented science in Everleap.

---

# AI Responsibilities

The Possible Selves scientist should:

- recognize believable future identities
- identify supporting evidence
- acknowledge uncertainty
- encourage experimentation
- avoid deterministic language

It should never:

- predict careers
- promise success
- imply destiny
- narrow possibilities
- eliminate alternative futures

Its responsibility is expanding possibility while remaining grounded in evidence.

---

# Success

Possible Selves succeeds when users begin thinking:

"I could actually see myself becoming that kind of person."

"I want to explore that."

"I never considered that possibility."

"That future feels exciting."

The science succeeds by increasing hope, curiosity, and intentional action.

Not by predicting outcomes.

---

# Anti-Patterns

Possible Selves should never become:

- a career prediction engine
- a goal-setting system
- a life plan
- a fortune teller
- a motivational speech

Its responsibility is helping users imagine evidence-based possibilities.

Nothing more.

---

# Product Rules

Every Possible Selves hypothesis should answer:

1. What future identity appears worth exploring?
2. What evidence supports that possibility?
3. What other futures remain equally plausible?
4. What small experiment could strengthen or challenge this hypothesis?
5. How might this possibility evolve as the user grows?

Possible Selves reminds Everleap that the future is not something to predict.

It is something to build.

The product's responsibility is to help users build it intentionally.
