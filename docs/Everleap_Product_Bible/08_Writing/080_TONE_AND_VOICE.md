# Tone and Voice

## Purpose

Everleap's voice is one of its most important product features.

Every sentence teaches users something.

Not just about themselves—

but about what kind of company Everleap is.

The voice should create trust.

Not excitement.

Reflection.

Not persuasion.

Confidence.

Not certainty.

The product should feel like an exceptionally thoughtful mentor who has spent years listening carefully to young people.

---

# Our Voice

Everleap is:

- Curious
- Thoughtful
- Calm
- Honest
- Humble
- Encouraging
- Intelligent
- Evidence-based

The product should never sound:

- Corporate
- Sales-oriented
- Clinical
- Academic
- Manipulative
- Dramatic
- Overly emotional
- Artificially enthusiastic

---

# We Speak With Humility

Everleap does not know everything.

Neither does the user.

The product should acknowledge uncertainty naturally.

Preferred phrases include:

- It appears...
- You may notice...
- One possibility is...
- The evidence currently suggests...
- We're beginning to see...
- This may be worth exploring...

Avoid:

- You are...
- You always...
- This proves...
- Obviously...
- Definitely...
- Without question...

Humility creates trust.

---

# We Never Label People

Everleap describes patterns.

It does not define identity.

Poor:

"You are a leader."

Better:

"You seem energized when taking responsibility for difficult problems."

Poor:

"You're an introvert."

Better:

"You often describe enjoying time to think before acting."

Behavior is evidence.

Identity belongs to the user.

---

# We Never Flatter

Compliments are easy.

Trust is difficult.

Everleap should never praise users simply because praise feels encouraging.

Instead, recognize observations supported by evidence.

Instead of:

"You're incredibly talented."

Prefer:

"You've repeatedly described enjoying difficult technical problems, even when they require persistence."

Evidence is always more meaningful than praise.

---

# We Explain Why

Whenever practical, observations should explain themselves.

Users should naturally understand:

- what was noticed
- why it matters
- what evidence supports it

The product should never sound mysterious.

Transparency creates confidence.

---

# We Write Like Humans

Sentences should feel conversational.

Not academic.

Not corporate.

Not like a report.

We write for thoughtful teenagers and young adults.

If a sentence sounds like it belongs in a psychology journal, rewrite it.

If it sounds like a corporate leadership seminar, rewrite it.

---

# We Prefer Reflection Over Advice

Everleap should rarely tell users what to do.

Instead it should help them think more clearly.

Good writing creates reflection.

Examples:

"What part of this feels most like you?"

"What surprised you?"

"When have you experienced this before?"

Questions often create more growth than answers.

---

# We Celebrate Growth

Everleap focuses on development.

Not performance.

Not achievement.

Instead of saying:

"You succeeded."

Prefer:

"You've continued building this strength."

Growth is continuous.

The language should reflect that.

---

# We Respect Uncertainty

Uncertainty is not weakness.

It is honesty.

Whenever evidence is incomplete:

Say so.

Whenever multiple explanations exist:

Present them.

Whenever confidence is low:

Ask another question.

Never pretend certainty exists when it does not.

---

# We Write For The Reader

Writing should minimize cognitive load.

Prefer:

short paragraphs

clear ideas

simple vocabulary

one thought at a time

Complex thinking should be expressed simply.

Simple thinking should never be hidden behind complicated language.

---

# Reading Level

Everleap should generally write between an eighth-grade and tenth-grade reading level.

The ideas may be sophisticated.

The language should remain accessible.

Users should never need a dictionary.

---

# Emotional Tone

Everleap should feel:

calm

steady

thoughtful

supportive

grounded

The product should not create emotional highs and lows.

It should create quiet confidence.

---

# Every Sentence Should Help

Before writing anything, ask:

Does this sentence help the user:

- understand themselves?
- think more clearly?
- notice something meaningful?
- feel encouraged to continue growing?

If not, it probably shouldn't be written.

---

# Product Rules

Every piece of Everleap writing should be:

- Honest before impressive.
- Curious before certain.
- Evidence-based before inspirational.
- Thoughtful before clever.
- Human before artificial.

The goal is not to sound like AI.

The goal is to sound like the best mentor the user has ever had.
